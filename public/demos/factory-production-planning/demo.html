Out of Stock — Invisible Cost

~2.7%

OOS-to-sales ratio in baseline months. Lost margin on every unserved order — never visible to the planner at plan time. The system had no mechanism to price the cost of getting it wrong.

Working Capital — Tied Up Unnecessarily

3 weeks+

Stock being held on short shelf-life SKUs with low seasonal demand. Planners kept lines running to avoid shift cost — without seeing the working capital accumulating in slow-moving inventory.

16 Data Sources. Zero Integration.

SAP stock, procurement batches, demand plans, downtime schedules, BOM data, shift agreements — all consulted separately, manually. No single source of optimised truth. Errors compound silently.

No Constraint Enforcement

Raw material lead times, union shift scenarios, minimum order quantities, warehouse capacity caps — all enforced by memory and convention, not by any systematic check. Violations discovered after the fact.

Act 4 — Scenario: Zero-Touch Clean Week
The Optimiser at Work
A normal production week. All 16 data sources fresh. No downtime. No RM constraints. Watch the MILP solve from data to shift-sequenced plan — automatically.
1
Input Validation & Pre-Processing
All 16 datasets pass the DE gate. 19 model input tabs assembled. Sets, coefficients, and time periods extracted for the solver.
✓ 19 tabs validated · 42 SKUs in scope · 9-week horizon
2
Reward Function Construction
For each order and time period, a reward value is computed — highest when produced at the right week (minimises OOS), penalised for earliness (working capital) or lateness (lost sales).
✓ 1,764 order × time-period reward values computed
3
Top Level MILP Solve
Solver maximises: Σ reward × production — shift costs — changeover costs — excess stock penalties. Subject to equipment capacity, raw material batches, union shift scenarios, min order quantities, warehouse ceiling.
✓ Optimal solution found · Line 3: Scenario 2 · Line 4: Scenario 3 · 12 shifts selected
4
Changeover Estimation
Pack-size changeovers and flavour changeovers estimated per line per week. Time carved out of available shift hours to ensure bottom-level schedule is feasible.
✓ Line 3: 3 pack changes · 7 flavour changes · Line 4: 2 pack · 5 flavour
5
Bottom Level Scheduling
Within each selected shift, orders are sequenced greedily to minimise changeover time. Downtimes, cleaning cycles, and raw material validity windows respected. Spare capacity checked for OOS fill opportunities.
✓ Full shift schedule generated · 0 constraint violations · 2 OOS fill orders added
6
Output Export to SharePoint
Two plan variants (with and without RM constraint) copied to SharePoint. Planner reviews by 9am. Total elapsed time from pipeline start: 22 minutes.
✓ Plan ready 08:52am — 22 min total · Zero manual input required
