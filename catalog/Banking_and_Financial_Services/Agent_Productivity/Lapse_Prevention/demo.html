Act 1 — The Problem

80,000 policies lost every year.
No one saw it coming.

In life insurance, a policy lapses when a customer misses a payment. Once lapsed, fewer than 1 in 10 are ever reinstated. The premium is gone — permanently. And the old way of managing this? Entirely reactive.

The real cost of "brute force"

Advisors spend equal time on every overdue policy — with no intelligence about which ones are truly at risk, and which will self-correct. High-value policies at breaking point receive the same attention as stable ones. The result: the most critical interventions happen too late, or not at all.
